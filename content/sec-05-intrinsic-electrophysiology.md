# Intrinsic Electrophysiology

```{authorship-explorer}
Section 5 · Intrinsic Electrophysiology
```

Section {ref}`sec-morphology` closed on the observation that VIP morphology is not a single *bipolar* stereotype but at least three co-existing axes — bipolar/bitufted versus basket versus multipolar — with regional variation layered on top. Intrinsic electrophysiology tells a structurally similar story: a cell class that is often summarised as *irregular-spiking, high-input-resistance* in fact partitions into partially replicable sub-fingerprints whose number, membership, and dominant conductances depend on which VIP-expressing population is recorded and how the resulting cells are clustered. This section adjudicates five points: (i) how many electrophysiological subtypes of VIP interneurons have been reported, (ii) how the passive membrane properties behave across labs, (iii) which conductances are argued to be dominant, (iv) whether *irregular-spiking* is a universal VIP property or a subtype feature, and (v) where within-family physiology grades continuously between molecularly defined branches — mirroring the Patch-seq continua surfaced in Section {ref}`sec-molecular-identity`.

## 5.1 Firing patterns: irregular, bursting, adapting, and continuous

The canonical description of a VIP interneuron as an *irregular-spiking* (IS) cell traces to the rat neocortex, where {cite:t}`Cauli1997` defined irregular-spiking interneurons as cells that fire bursts of action potentials at irregular frequency, distinct from fast-spiking and regular-spiking nonpyramidal cells; later work extended the label to the VIP/CR-bipolar class that dominates supragranular layers {cite:p}`Cauli2014,Lee2013,Rudy2010`. Reviews written on top of this evidence concluded that the most frequently observed VIP interneurons are the bipolar or bitufted neurons with an irregular-spiking firing pattern {cite:p}`Lee2013`, and that calretinin-overlapping VIP cells exhibit a mixture of adapting, bursting, and irregular behaviours within a single class {cite:p}`Cauli2014`. Several early mouse studies inherited the categorical IS label: {cite:t}`Vucurovic2010` characterised 5-HT3A/VIP bipolar cells as a distinct electrophysiological subtype in mouse somatosensory cortex, while {cite:t}`Pohlkamp2013` reported that VIP-expressing cells in rat barrel cortex form a cluster with *adapting and/or bursting* firing — a description that already sat uneasily with the monolithic IS gloss. A concise survey by {cite:t}`Rudy2010` allowed multiple VIP firing phenotypes tied to different marker combinations, foreshadowing the present diversity.

Modern, larger-sample clustering studies have not converged on a single number of VIP electrophysiological subtypes ({numref}`fig-sec5-etype-count`). In mouse primary somatosensory cortex layer 2/3, {cite:t}`Goff2019` sampled 273 VIP-Cre neurons and forced a two-way split into irregular-spiking (IS) and continuous-adapting (CA) types, using the resulting labels to demonstrate that Scn1a+/- dysfunction is restricted to the IS subset, with CA cells essentially normal and the IS firing pattern dependent on KCNQ/M-current. In the same cortical area and layer, {cite:t}`Bogaj2025a` applied PCA and hierarchical clustering without a fixed k and recovered three e-types, with ~68 % of cells falling into a single GABA$_B$-receptor-sensitive *type 1* cluster and the remaining cells split between two smaller clusters differentially modulated by baclofen. In mouse entorhinal cortex, {cite:t}`Ferrante2016` characterised VIP interneurons alongside five other molecularly identified classes, with hierarchical clustering predicting molecular identity at ~81% accuracy; within-VIP heterogeneity was evident but the study did not estimate a stable VIP subtype count. A layer-1-specific study by {cite:t}`Meng2023` partitioned the broader L1 interneuron population into four feature-defined classes (canopy, NGFC, α7, and VIP cells) in which VIP forms one class, and reported that all 13 VIP cells recorded carried an irregular-firing (IR or IR2) feature — with 8/13 specifically classified as IR2 — so that within the L1 VIP class there is no binary IS-versus-non-IS split rather than four within-VIP subtypes. Superclass-level studies that include VIP cells without isolating them give yet larger numbers: {cite:t}`Emmenegger2018` partitioned rat layer-4 non-fast-spiking interneurons into five e-types with three distinct VIP-overlapping clusters, and the Patch-seq study of {cite:t}`Fuzik2015` identified five I-type clusters within cortical CCK+/GAD67+ L1 interneurons with VIP cells distributed across several of them. Regional and laminar surveys converge with this picture: {cite:t}`Martinez2017` reported four MEC superficial-layer interneuron clusters including a VIP-containing slow-firing group, and {cite:t}`Kay2014` identified at least five functional interneuron classes in the anterior olfactory nucleus, again including VIP-overlapping cells. The broader CCK/VGLUT3/CB1 literature recovers similarly heterogeneous VIP/CCK/CR cells in human and rodent samples {cite:p}`Somogyi2025,Varga2015,Kamalova2024`.

Two points of disagreement should be logged before leaving firing patterns. First, whether VIP firing is *dominantly* irregular or *dominantly* adapting is a sustained cross-lab conflict: {cite:t}`Meng2023` assert that irregular firing is the single dominant feature of L1 VIP cells and observe that *heterogeneous VIP cells can exhibit onset bursting, spike-frequency adaptation, or irregular firing within the same cell*, whereas {cite:t}`Pohlkamp2013` assigned cortical VIP cells as a whole to adapting/bursting rather than IS, and {cite:t}`Kanigowski2024` recently identified a distinct accommodating (VIP-AC) firing cluster in barrel-cortex L4 whose intrinsic excitability is modulated by learning. Second, whether VIP cells carry a *distinctive* electrophysiological fingerprint at all is contested: {cite:t}`Bogaj2023` found that GABA$_A$-R-mediated inhibition decreases VIP intrinsic excitability but leaves SST cells unchanged — implying distinct intrinsic regulation — yet their resting electrical parameters overlap substantially with SST cells recorded in identical conditions. The Patch-seq synthesis of {cite:t}`Scala2020` describes VIP as a *distinct and essentially non-overlapping* morpho-electric family at the broad transcriptomic level, but notes that individual transcriptomic types within the VIP family are not electrophysiologically separable from one another, so that *distinctive* and *heterogeneous* are simultaneously true at different levels of aggregation. Confirmatory evidence that VIP cells sit at the distinctive end comes from {cite:t}`Rooy2021`, who report the highest input resistance of any simulated cortical class, and from {cite:t}`Lee2013`, who note significantly higher resting membrane potentials and input resistance in 5-HT3AR interneurons (which include VIP) relative to PYR/PV/SOM neighbours. We return to this question after passing through Rin, AP shape, and conductance repertoire.


## 5.2 Passive membrane properties, AP shape, and sag

Input resistance (R$_{in}$) is the most frequently reported intrinsic parameter for VIP interneurons, and it is where the apparent between-lab spread is largest ({numref}`fig-sec5-rin-distribution`). Once the entries are stratified by molecular identity and age, however, much of the spread resolves into within-lab biology rather than between-lab noise. In adult mouse somatosensory cortex L2/3, VIP-Cre-pure recordings cluster below ~400 MΩ: {cite:t}`Bogaj2023` report 269 ± 63 MΩ in 17 cells from 17 mice, and {cite:t}`Simacek2025a` report 354 ± 136 MΩ in P30+ animals. The same VIP-Cre cohort recorded neonatally yields substantially higher values (626 ± 176 MΩ at P9), with {cite:t}`Simacek2025a` reporting a parallel developmental decrease in membrane time constant from ~36 ms at P9 to ~12 ms at P30+, indicating maturation towards faster signal integration consistent with the postnatal programme summarised in Section {ref}`sec-development`. When a molecularly narrower VIP/ChAT population is recorded at room temperature, {cite:t}`Dudai2020` find an intermediate 340 ± 60 MΩ, and when the VIP+/CR+ I-S3 subset is isolated in CA1 stratum oriens/alveus, {cite:t}`Michaud2024b` report 517 ± 37 MΩ. Qualitative statements in the broader literature are consistent with these values: {cite:t}`Lee2013` describe significantly higher R$_{in}$ in 5-HT3AR interneurons relative to PYR/PV/SOM neighbours, {cite:t}`Thompson2023a` describe VIP superficial-layer interneurons as having *extremely high* membrane resistance (a verbal claim that sits well above the quantitative entries retained here), and computational work of {cite:t}`Rooy2021` gives VIP the highest R$_{in}$ of any interneuron class in a simulated cortical column. Two studies that the figure redesign removed are worth flagging: a nNOS+ cluster reported by {cite:t}`Perrenoud2012b` and the EC3 cluster of {cite:t}`Emmenegger2018` both have denominators that include non-VIP cells, and the numerical means (360 MΩ and 151 MΩ respectively) cannot be interpreted as VIP-specific.

Read jointly, the R$_{in}$ evidence supports two complementary conclusions. First, VIP interneurons are, as a class, high-R$_{in}$ relative to local PYR and PV neurons {cite:p}`Lee2013,Rooy2021,Thompson2023a`, which — combined with their typical resting potential around -66 mV in the VIP/ChAT subclass {cite:p}`Dudai2020` — places them among the cells most sensitive to small modulatory currents. Second, the quantitative spread from ~270 to ~630 MΩ seen across adult and neonatal recordings is not a failure to replicate but a window on molecular and developmental identity: VIP/ChAT cells sit near the bulk-VIP mean {cite:p}`Dudai2020`, hippocampal VIP/CR I-S3 cells sit clearly above it {cite:p}`Michaud2024b`, and R$_{in}$ drops steeply during postnatal maturation within the same cohort {cite:p}`Simacek2025a`. The conflict between {cite:t}`Dudai2020`'s moderate R$_{in}$ (~340 MΩ) and the *extremely high* descriptor used by {cite:t}`Thompson2023a` is therefore not a strict contradiction; it reflects a difference between point-estimate measurement in a defined molecular subclass and qualitative claims over a broader VIP population.

Action-potential waveform and after-hyperpolarisation (AHP) parameters show a similar pattern of subtype-resolved consistency. Within the VIP/ChAT branch, {cite:t}`Dudai2020` report a threshold of approximately -48 mV and a membrane time constant of 26 ms, consistent with slow integration. In L2/3 VIP-Cre cells, {cite:t}`Simacek2025a` document a progressive narrowing of AP half-width and deepening of fast AHP during postnatal maturation, matching the conductance maturation programme referenced in Section {ref}`sec-development`. In a disease context, {cite:t}`Michaud2024b` observe elongated APs and reduced firing rates in I-S3 cells of 3xTg-AD mice relative to age-matched controls — implicating VIP cells in Alzheimer-associated circuit dysfunction even though the baseline cell shape is preserved — and {cite:t}`Kanigowski2024` describe AP-shape changes in VIP-AC cells after pseudoconditioning. Whether VIP cells display a characteristic sag/Ih signature is more disputed than commonly assumed. {cite:t}`Fuzik2015` describe a clear Ih-driven sag in a subset of L1 CCK+ I-types, {cite:t}`Moreno2026` show that medial prefrontal L1b VIP cells have a constitutively active Ih that, together with Kir, sets their resting potential and membrane time constant, and {cite:t}`Herstel2024` report that HCN channels are *downregulated* after synaptic potentiation in VIP/reelin stratum-radiatum cells — the opposite direction to pyramidal neurons. Because HCN-mediated sag is layer- and subtype-dependent {cite:p}`Moreno2026,Herstel2024`, we treat *presence of sag* as a subtype-discriminating feature rather than a canonical VIP property; this is a direct setup for the Kv3-versus-HCN debate in the next subsection.


## 5.3 Ion-channel repertoire: Nav1.1, KCNQ/M, Kv3, HCN/Kir, T-type Ca, and Kcnt1

The question of *which* ion channels dominate VIP intrinsic behaviour has crystallised into four partially disagreeing lines of evidence. The first centres on Nav1.1 and M-current. {cite:t}`Goff2019` show that Scn1a+/- mice exhibit reduced maximal steady-state firing frequency in VIP-INs (67 ± 2 Hz WT vs. 48 ± 3 Hz in heterozygotes, *n* = 150/123; p = 3×10⁻⁶), consistent with loss of Na$^+$ current carried by Nav1.1. Crucially, the same study demonstrates that this dysfunction is restricted to the IS subtype, while the CA subtype is essentially normal, and that the IS firing pattern is determined by KCNQ-mediated M-current — blocking M-current in CA cells converts them to an IS-like pattern. {cite:t}`Liebergall2024` extended this logic by recording Ndnf-INs in Scn1a+/- mice and finding no parallel Nav1.1-dependent deficit, implying that the Nav1.1 requirement is a specific property of VIP-IS cells rather than a general CGE-derived feature (cross-reference Section {ref}`sec-development`). {cite:t}`Hammer2025` reviews Nav1.1 within the broader SCN1A epilepsy literature, and {cite:t}`Scalmani2023` — working in mouse entorhinal PV, CCK, and SOM interneurons rather than VIP — characterises Nav1.1 biophysics in parallel CGE-adjacent classes, and {cite:t}`Shore2024` show that Kcnt1 gain-of-function alters cortical firing through a distinct sodium-activated K$^+$ pathway. Together these results argue that Nav-KCNQ coupling is the dominant regulator of IS VIP firing, but specifically of *IS* VIP firing — not all VIP cells.

A second line focuses on Kv3 channels. Computational modelling by {cite:t}`GuetMcCreight2016` produced biophysically detailed IS3 VIP/CR models in CA1 that require Kv3-family currents in the soma and proximal dendrites to reproduce the short half-width, deep fast-AHP, and high-frequency firing observed experimentally. Kv3 is a canonical fast-spiking PV marker whose role in shaping the fast-AHP and high-frequency firing of non-VIP interneuron classes is reviewed in {cite:t}`Zemel2023` (primary data in zebra finch arcopallium, cited here only for its Kv3 review framing). {cite:t}`Faulkner2024` and {cite:t}`Chen2023a` extend the Kv3 story into disease-relevant contexts where Kv3 expression itself is modulated, and {cite:t}`Hu2023a` report that Tsc1 loss in VIP-lineage interneurons yields more VIP+ cells with enhanced excitability, plausibly downstream of altered voltage-gated K$^+$ channel expression. In contrast, a third, HCN/Kir-dominant account comes from {cite:t}`Moreno2026`, who show that layer 1b VIP cells in mPFC possess constitutively active Ih *and* Kir conductances that jointly regulate passive properties: blocking Kir with BaCl$_2$ depolarises the resting potential, increases R$_{in}$ and membrane time constant, and reduces rebound firing, while blocking Ih with ZD7288 hyperpolarises the cell and slows its kinetics. {cite:t}`Herstel2024` further complicate the HCN story by showing that HCN channels are downregulated after plasticity induction in VIP/reelin cells, a direction opposite to pyramidal neurons, indicating that HCN is both present and dynamically regulated in at least some VIP subtypes.

The fourth line concerns T-type Ca and D-type K$^+$ channels as drivers of bursting. {cite:t}`Cattani2024` build a biophysical model of VIP bursting — parameterised on rodent basolateral amygdala type-I VIP interneurons rather than cortex — that requires a slowly inactivating D-type K$^+$ current to produce the observed onset bursts with subsequent adaptation, and {cite:t}`Chamberland2010` document T-type Ca-dependent low-threshold spikes in VIP/CR+ cells of the dentate gyrus that support rebound burst firing. Disease-model work of and {cite:t}`Liebergall2024` implicates additional Na$^+$/K$^+$ channel defects in VIP-associated epilepsies, and clinical reviews {cite:p}`Hammer2025` align these findings with SCN1A loss-of-function phenotypes. The reconciled reading is that Kv3 and HCN/Kir are not in direct competition across studies: Kv3 dominates the hippocampal and cortical *fast-AHP / high-frequency* VIP subtypes {cite:p}`GuetMcCreight2016`, while HCN/Kir dominates slower, high-R$_{in}$ layer-1/1b subtypes {cite:p}`Moreno2026,Herstel2024`, and T-type Ca or D-type K$^+$ become visible in bursting subsets {cite:p}`Chamberland2010,Cattani2024`. What the data do not yet support is a single canonical VIP ion-channel fingerprint, and any claim to such a fingerprint should be flagged as a composition-of-subtypes artefact.

Several ancillary claims round out the conductance picture. {cite:t}`Ghaderi2018` and show that Nav1.1 haploinsufficiency produces distinctive input-output curve shifts in VIP-like interneurons across rodent and non-rodent preparations, {cite:t}`Cauli2014` note a persistent sodium current that contributes to irregularity in CR/VIP bipolar cells, and {cite:t}`Berg2021` describe Patch-seq-defined transcriptomic gradients of Kv and HCN subunit expression along the human VIP family. In the sensory cortex, {cite:t}`Qi2022` show that the ACh-induced depolarisation in layer-4 non-fast-spiking interneurons, including VIP+-like cells, is mediated primarily by M1 and/or M3/5 muscarinic receptors with only a minor M2/4 contribution, occasionally driving spontaneous firing; these cholinergic effects sit on top of the intrinsic channel complement and interact with the HCN/Kir-regulated resting potential documented by {cite:t}`Moreno2026`. The IS-fraction stratification taken up in the next subsection inherits all three conductance lines: a cell classified as IS under a Petilla-style protocol may be IS because of its M-current (Goff), its Kv3 complement (Guet-McCreight), or its dynamics of Ih/Kir (Moreno), and these *need not be the same cell*.


## 5.4 Subtype-specific physiology and cross-lab replication

We can now ask which of the subtype-level claims replicate across labs. Three subtypes recur in the evidence and warrant separate treatment.

*VIP/ChAT as a distinct branch.* The VIP/ChAT population is the only VIP subset for which R$_{in}$, resting potential, and AP shape have been matched across at least three studies ({numref}`fig-sec5-rin-distribution`). {cite:t}`Dudai2020` provide the canonical cortical profile (340 ± 60 MΩ; -66 mV resting; threshold ~-48 mV; time constant 26 ms), {cite:t}`Trojan2025` review the cholinergic/VIP co-expression phenotype — although their primary data are from mouse lateral septum rather than neocortex, and {cite:t}`Perrenoud2012a` localise the class to superficial layers across rodent cortex. {cite:t}`vonEngelhardt2007` famously argued that the VIP/CR/ChAT bipolar population in cortical L2/3 is *non-GABAergic* and does not produce measurable GABAergic output — a claim that is challenged by more recent Patch-seq and paired-recording work {cite:p}`Du2025` showing that calretinin-overlapping VIP bipolar cells are in fact GABAergic accommodating or irregular-spiking interneurons. Whether the 2007 finding reflects a rare GABA-null subset or a method-specific false negative remains unresolved; we return to it in Section {ref}`sec-synaptic-connectivity` with paired-recording connectivity data. Within the intrinsic window of this section, the {cite:t}`Dudai2020` profile for VIP/ChAT cells is the best-anchored VIP subtype.

*Hippocampal VIP+/CR+ I-S3 cells.* The I-S3 cell (so-named for its type-3-interneuron-targeting axon in CA1) is the best-characterised hippocampal VIP subtype. {cite:t}`Chamberland2010` first described its dendritic inhibition of O-LM cells, and {cite:t}`Michaud2024b` quantify its high R$_{in}$ (517 ± 37 MΩ) and narrow AP. {cite:t}`GuetMcCreight2016` reproduce the I-S3 phenotype in biophysical models with Kv3 channels in soma and proximal dendrites, and {cite:t}`Tyan2014` extend the class to a monoaminergic-responsive VIP subclass in hippocampus. Cross-lab agreement on I-S3 intrinsic parameters is higher than for any other VIP subtype, and it is a useful calibration point when judging the spread seen in bulk cortical VIP-Cre recordings.

*Cortical IS versus CA versus accommodating types.* This axis contains the most evidence and the most disagreement. {cite:t}`Goff2019` force a binary IS/CA split and find dysfunction in one arm; {cite:t}`Bogaj2025a` find three types under unsupervised clustering with ~68 % IS-like (GABA$_B$-sensitive) cells; {cite:t}`Meng2023` report that every L1 VIP cell they recorded (13/13) carried an irregular-firing feature, though VIP here is a single class rather than four VIP subtypes; {cite:t}`Badrinarayanan2021` describe ~60 % irregular-spiking and ~30 % CNA cells in mouse PFC VIP populations; {cite:t}`Kanigowski2024` identify a distinct VIP-AC accommodating cluster in L4 whose excitability changes with learning. The fraction of cells classified as IS therefore varies by layer (L1 → near-universal; L2/3 → 40–68 %; PFC → 60 %) and by classification pipeline ({numref}`fig-sec5-is-fraction`). We emphasise that the cross-study percentages are *not* directly comparable because the operational definitions differ — a Petilla-style categorical IS label is not the same as an unsupervised-cluster type-1 membership or a feature-based IR category — so the bars in {numref}`fig-sec5-is-fraction` must be read as stratified descriptions rather than as a scatter around a single truth.

A parallel strand of evidence reinforces the layered nature of these subdivisions. Patch-seq studies that jointly measure transcriptome and physiology find VIP families populated by morpho-electric *continua* rather than sharp categorical boundaries: {cite:t}`Scala2020` map mouse motor cortex Patch-seq cells onto a VIP axis where t-type identity tracks smooth electrophysiological gradients, {cite:t}`Scala2019` extend this across layers in somatosensory cortex, {cite:t}`Lee2021` report high-fidelity electrophysiological, morphological, and transcriptomic alignment within the VIP branch, and {cite:t}`Gouwens2020b` conclude that at least 28 met-types are congruent across the three modalities but that the VIP family contains graded rather than discrete boundaries. This is exactly the pattern previewed in Section {ref}`sec-molecular-identity`: the VIP branch is coherent at the level of family membership but *continuous within the family at electrophysiological granularity*. Cross-species work of {cite:t}`Bernaerts2023,Bernaerts2025` and {cite:t}`Tripathy2018` re-derives this conclusion on independent cohorts; {cite:t}`Berg2021` and {cite:t}`Berg2021` extend it to human cortex. The practical consequence is that *the number of VIP e-types* is a projection of a continuous space onto a chosen clustering k, which must be reported alongside the number.

Regional and cross-species variation layers additional diversity. {cite:t}`Badrinarayanan2021` document medial-entorhinal-cortex gradients in VIP intrinsic properties along the dorsoventral axis; {cite:t}`Martinez2017` describe four MEC superficial-layer interneuron clusters with VIP-overlapping slow-firing cells; {cite:t}`Kay2014` identify VIP-containing interneurons among at least five functional classes in the anterior olfactory nucleus. Human and primate recordings by {cite:t}`Varga2015`,, {cite:t}`Krienen2020`, and show that human VIP/CR/VGLUT3/CB1 cells carry the same firing-pattern scaffold as mouse cells but with slower AP kinetics and higher R$_{in}$, consistent with the broader interneuron species-scaling literature. Disease-model work of {cite:t}`Hu2023a` (Tsc1 loss → enhanced VIP excitability), {cite:t}`Goff2019,Liebergall2024` (Nav1.1 epilepsies), {cite:t}`Michaud2024b` (Alzheimer model), and {cite:t}`Shore2024` (Kcnt1 GOF) shows that the VIP intrinsic profile is not fixed: specific channelopathies shift specific VIP subtypes, again arguing against a single canonical VIP fingerprint. Computational and modelling studies {cite:p}`Rooy2021,Cattani2024,GuetMcCreight2016,Ferrante2016` operationalise the firing-pattern and R$_{in}$ properties reviewed here and provide the substrate for the parameter choices explored in Section {ref}`sec-computational-models`.

## 5.5 Ties to development, molecular identity, and computational models

Three cross-section ties deserve explicit flagging before closing this section. First, the developmental trajectory anchored by {cite:t}`Simacek2025a` — with R$_{in}$ dropping from ~626 MΩ at P9 to ~354 MΩ by P30+ and membrane time constant shortening from ~36 ms to ~12 ms over the same window — is mechanistically continuous with the postnatal channel-expression programme reviewed in Section {ref}`sec-development`. {cite:t}`Pohlkamp2013` note that VIP cells migrate tangentially from the caudal ganglionic eminence and settle primarily in superficial layers, and {cite:t}`Frazer2017` show that the 5-HT3AR expression characteristic of the CGE lineage is present at birth and persists into adulthood, setting the substrate for the adult intrinsic fingerprint measured by {cite:t}`Bogaj2023,Dudai2020`.,, and add complementary evidence that cortical VIP cell intrinsic maturation is activity-dependent, depending on sensory drive and on neuromodulator signalling during early postnatal life. Second, the Patch-seq mapping onto transcriptomic taxonomy {cite:p}`Scala2020,Lee2021,Gouwens2020b,Tripathy2018,Fuzik2015` replicates the continuum structure we see here, tying the e-type variation in this section to the molecular gradients of Section {ref}`sec-molecular-identity`. Third, biophysical and network models {cite:p}`GuetMcCreight2016,Cattani2024,Rooy2021,Ferrante2016` depend sensitively on the very parameters this section has catalogued: the IS-fraction bars in {numref}`fig-sec5-is-fraction` and the R$_{in}$ facets in {numref}`fig-sec5-rin-distribution` are the inputs that set the disinhibitory impact of VIP in Section {ref}`sec-computational-models`. Reporting a single lumped VIP cell in such models — as several early column models did — misses the subtype resolution that this section argues is essential, and we return to this point in the computational synthesis.

It is also worth recording what is *not* supported by the present evidence. The claim that VIP cells are universally fast-spiking-adjacent in Kv3 behaviour is not supported: Kv3 expression is prominent in I-S3 and some cortical IS cells {cite:p}`GuetMcCreight2016` but not across all VIP subtypes, and HCN/Kir-dominated L1b cells {cite:p}`Moreno2026` sit well outside any Kv3-centric description. The claim that R$_{in}$ values above ~1 GΩ are characteristic of adult VIP cells is not supported by any adult VIP-Cre-pure cohort in the evidence package; the only near-gigaohm values are developmental (P9–P14) or qualitative descriptors rather than measured means {cite:p}`Simacek2025a,Thompson2023a`. And the {cite:t}`vonEngelhardt2007` claim of a non-GABAergic VIP/CR/ChAT cortical population is challenged but not definitively overturned by more recent work {cite:p}`Du2025`; whether it represents a rare subset, a methodological false negative, or a regional idiosyncrasy is an open question that requires paired-recording data from Section {ref}`sec-synaptic-connectivity`. Finally, whether VIP cells have a single *characteristic* sag/Ih property is contested rather than settled: large sag is documented in some L1 and L1b cells {cite:p}`Moreno2026,Fuzik2015` but not in the bulk cortical L2/3 VIP-Cre recordings of {cite:t}`Bogaj2023` or the VIP/ChAT cohort of {cite:t}`Dudai2020`. Readers seeking canonical values should consult the subtype facet they are modelling rather than the class aggregate.

## 5.6 Summary and bridge to connectivity

Intrinsic physiology, like morphology, carves VIP interneurons into at least three partially replicable sub-fingerprints — a VIP/ChAT branch, a hippocampal I-S3 branch, and cortical IS/CA/AC axes — but the *specific subdivisions* reported depend on which VIP population is recorded, which layer, and which classification pipeline is used. Four disagreements survive stratification and should be treated as open questions: (i) the number of cortical VIP e-types (2 vs 3 vs 4 depending on clustering policy, {cite:t}`Goff2019,Bogaj2025a,Meng2023`), (ii) whether *irregular-spiking* is universal or a subtype feature ({cite:t}`Meng2023` vs {cite:t}`Pohlkamp2013,Kanigowski2024`), (iii) which conductances dominate — Nav1.1/KCNQ vs Kv3 vs HCN/Kir ({cite:t}`Goff2019,GuetMcCreight2016,Moreno2026`), and (iv) whether VIP+/CR+/ChAT bipolar cells are GABAergic in situ ({cite:t}`vonEngelhardt2007` vs ). None of these conflicts are artefacts of small samples; they reflect operational differences between laboratories and, in several cases, genuine subtype biology that pooling discards. The synthesis from Patch-seq {cite:p}`Scala2020,Gouwens2020b,Fuzik2015,Cadwell2015` argues that the VIP family is molecularly coherent but electrophysiologically continuous within the family — which is the same conclusion we drew from the morphology of Section {ref}`sec-morphology`.

A cell's intrinsic physiology, however, only constrains what it *can* fire in isolation; it says nothing directly about what it *does* to the rest of the circuit. The {cite:t}`vonEngelhardt2007`-versus-{cite:t}`Du2025` conflict above is the sharpest case in point: two descriptions of the same molecular population, divided not by intrinsic parameters but by whether the cell actually transmits GABA at its axon terminals. That question cannot be adjudicated from intrinsic recordings alone; it requires paired recordings, optogenetic activation of identified axons, and quantitative connectivity maps. Section {ref}`sec-synaptic-connectivity` takes up exactly this question for VIP interneurons — quantifying the strength, kinetics, and target specificity of VIP-output synapses, and asking whether the alternative targets (PV, VIP-self, pyramidal) obscured by the canonical VIP → SST framing are in fact the majority of VIP output.
