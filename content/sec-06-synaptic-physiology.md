# Synaptic Properties and Connectivity

{ref}`sec-electrophysiology` showed that intrinsic electrophysiology partitions the VIP class into at least two axes — irregular-spiking bipolar versus adapting basket — but that the specific subdivisions disagree across laboratories and do not yet map cleanly onto canonical functional labels. The synapses those cells make, and the inputs those cells receive, are what translate that intrinsic heterogeneity into circuit behavior. This section asks what the field has actually measured about VIP synaptic output (to SST, PV, pyramidal and other VIP cells), VIP synaptic input (local, long-range, neuromodulatory), and intra-class electrical coupling, and where the canonical VIP→SST→PYR motif is supported by paired-recording or targeted-optogenetic evidence rather than propagated as a textbook schematic.

A guiding observation: the literature on VIP connectivity is dominated by a small number of landmark papers that tested the motif directly and a very large number of studies that cite those papers to justify a disinhibitory interpretation of downstream imaging or behavior. The difference between "tested" and "invoked" matters for how much evidential weight a given claim carries, and the two figures in this section are deliberately constructed to make that difference visible: one is a test-coverage matrix of VIP output connectivity across studies in {numref}`fig-sec6-target-coverage-matrix`, and the other is a source taxonomy of identified drive onto VIP in {numref}`fig-sec6-drive-source-taxonomy`. Both are presence/absence categorical matrices, not quantitative cross-study comparisons, because the extracted figure captions that populated this section's evidence package do not contain extractable connection-probability or IPSC-amplitude values in a commensurable form. That limitation is itself informative — for a "canonical" motif, the quantitative cross-study comparison is surprisingly thin.

## VIP output: how direct is the evidence for the canonical motif?

The test-coverage matrix in {numref}`fig-sec6-target-coverage-matrix` summarizes the six studies in this section's evidence base whose figure captions describe a direct test of VIP output connectivity — a paired recording with IPSC measurement in an identified postsynaptic cell, an optogenetic VIP activation with postsynaptic recording in SST, PV, or pyramidal cells, or an explicit implemented connection in a computational model. The rest of the 100 raw Section-6 entries describe morphology, in-vivo imaging, behavior, or input drive onto VIP and therefore cannot populate a VIP-output-test matrix. This is an artifact of the evidence extraction, not of the literature — but it is consistent with the broader pattern that a large fraction of "VIP connectivity" citations in the literature lean on two or three primary sources.

{cite:t}`Pfeffer2013` is the primary experimental demonstration for the preferential-SST targeting claim: by photostimulating VIP-Cre ChR2-expressing neurons while recording from Sst-, Pvalb-, and pyramidal cells in V1, the Scanziani lab showed that "not only do VIP-Cre cells inhibit Sst expressing cells but that Sst expressing cells represent their principal target" {cite:p}`Pfeffer2013`. This observation is the most-cited specific result in Section-6-relevant papers, and it is reproduced in the auditory cortex and mPFC by {cite:t}`Pi2013`, whose paired optogenetic + IPSC recordings showed equivalent preferential SST (and PV) targeting with closely matched short-term plasticity between the two regions {cite:p}`Pi2013`. Melzer and colleagues added a pharmacological handle via gastrin-releasing peptide (GRP), showing that GRP-driven recruitment of VIP cells produced IPSC-frequency increases in both SST and PVALB cells and a corresponding decrease in pyramidal-cell inhibition in auditory cortex — a three-cell-type replication of the Pfeffer and Pi schema in a new region with an independent trigger {cite:p}`Melzer2021`. These three studies are the core of the direct evidence for the canonical VIP→SST/PV→PYR motif; much of the remaining literature cites them.

The studies that do NOT meet the direct-test bar are equally instructive. {cite:t}`Zhang2014` inferred a top-down→VIP→SOM→PYR chain from disynaptic IPSC measurements during selective interneuron inactivation — a powerful experiment in which VIP inactivation was reported to increase center inhibition, consistent with loss of VIP→SOM disinhibition, but in which the VIP→SOM edge is inferred from a subtraction rather than measured as unitary connections {cite:p}`Zhang2014`. {cite:t}`Kastli2020` mapped laminar photostim drive ONTO superficial VIP+ and SST+ cells but did not test VIP outputs, so its appearance in a VIP-connectivity narrative should be read as input-side evidence only {cite:p}`Kastli2020`. {cite:t}`Shapiro2025` supplies a wiring schematic and photomodulation-latency measurements in V1 — the schematic references Karnani 2016 and Pfeffer 2013 but does not itself perform an output-connectivity test {cite:p}`Shapiro2025`. {cite:t}`Wagatsuma2025` is a spiking-network model of Pyr/PV/SOM/VIP populations in which the VIP→SOM connection is an implemented parameter drawn from prior literature rather than measured data (VIP→PV and VIP→Pyr are not included as modeled edges); model behavior therefore validates architectural plausibility rather than the underlying connectivity claim {cite:p}`Wagatsuma2025`. Collectively: of the six studies plotted in {numref}`fig-sec6-target-coverage-matrix`, two (Pi, Melzer) provide direct IPSC measurements across SST, PV, and Pyr targets; one (Pfeffer, which the evidence package assigns a separate role as the preferential-targeting demonstration) establishes the SST preference; one is inferential; one is schematic; one is a model. This is a small denominator for a claim that is propagated as settled background in the majority of the Section-6 literature.

The asymmetry matters for the VIP→PV edge specifically. Of the six output-test studies, three (Pi, Melzer, and Pfeffer as the landmark) report paired-recording or IPSC evidence that VIP inhibits PV cells; two (Zhang, Kastli) do not test it directly; and the Kuchibhotla and Pfeffer findings diverge on the relative magnitude of the PV component. {cite:t}`Pfeffer2013` reports that SST cells are the "principal target" of VIP output, with smaller IPSCs in PV cells {cite:p}`Pfeffer2013`. {cite:t}`Pi2013` finds equivalent VIP→SST and VIP→PV IPSCs in ACx and mPFC, with the VIP→PV component explicitly contributing to downstream disinhibition {cite:p}`Pi2013`. {cite:t}`Cauli2014` review the morphological literature and note that "VIP+ interneurons innervated PV+ interneurons, although it is debated whether there exists a preferential targeting in terms of putative PV+ subgroups" — a reminder that the SST-preference summary obscures subtype-specific PV connectivity that has not been resolved {cite:p}`Cauli2014`. Prönneke and colleagues, reconstructing VIP morphology in barrel cortex, emphasize that VIP cells form GAD-positive boutons but do not themselves express PV or SST, and conclude that VIP cells are "differentially integrated in as yet unidentified cortical circuits" — i.e., that the targeting pattern is heterogeneous within the VIP class {cite:p}`Pronneke2015`. The conflict here is not factual (all studies agree VIP→PV exists) but quantitative: how large, how consistent, and how subtype-selective the VIP→PV edge is remains unsettled across labs {cite:p}`Pfeffer2013,Pi2013,Cauli2014,Pronneke2015`.

## Direct VIP→pyramidal inhibition: a persistent and unresolved minority report

A substantial but unresolved strand of the literature reports that a minority of pyramidal cells receive monosynaptic IPSCs from VIP activation, complicating a strict disinhibition-only description. The conflict is explicit in the evidence package: {cite:t}`Pi2013` explicitly frames the downstream pyramidal signature as "excitation-inhibition-excitation," where the middle component is the disinhibitory phase rather than direct VIP→pyr inhibition {cite:p}`Pi2013`, and in-vivo imaging work has similarly described the pyramidal effect as largely indirect via PV and SOM interneurons. Against this, {cite:t}`Takesian2018` reports direct VIP→pyramidal inhibition in auditory-thalamocortical preparations — a reproducible minority component that is not predicted by a pure disinhibition model {cite:p}`Takesian2018`. The Pi-vs-Takesian opposition appears in this section's conflict ledger in {numref}`fig-sec6-target-coverage-matrix` the rows for Pi2013 list "no net direct inhibition highlighted" while downstream studies report monosynaptic IPSCs on pyramidal cells), and the evidence package marks all four as unresolved. {cite:t}`Kamigaki2017` navigates the tension by explicitly acknowledging both interpretations in the same study: "the overall decrease in activity could be mediated by direct inhibition of pyramidal neurons by VIP interneurons, whereas the context-dependent firing rate modulation could" be disinhibitory {cite:p}`Kamigaki2017`. The resolution the field is converging on is that VIP output is not exclusively to other interneurons — a minority but reproducible fraction of pyramidal cells receive monosynaptic VIP inhibition, and whether any given experiment surfaces that component depends on which pyramidal subpopulation is sampled and on the strength of the cortical state-dependent disinhibitory path that would otherwise swamp it {cite:p}`Pi2013,Takesian2018,Kuchibhotla2016`.

The VIP-basket / CCK-co-expressing subtype is an anatomical scaffold for the direct-inhibition finding. {cite:t}`Francavilla2018a` reports in CA1 that VIP basket cells, which co-express CCK, "can co-express cholecystokinin (CCK) and, in addition to targeting PC somata, can contact PV-positive BCs" — i.e., the same VIP+ cell class can exert both inhibitory (perisomatic onto pyramidal) and disinhibitory (onto PV basket cells) effects {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a`. Prönneke's morphological reconstructions in barrel cortex and the CCK-VIP literature summarized by {cite:t}`Apicella2022` are consistent: the VIP class contains at least one subclass (CCK-co-expressing basket cells) whose anatomical targeting pattern predicts direct pyramidal inhibition, and a second subclass (CR-co-expressing bipolar / interneuron-specific cells) whose targeting pattern predicts disinhibition {cite:p}`Pronneke2015,Apicella2022`. On this reading the Pi/Takesian/Garrett conflict is not a failure of replication — it is a consequence of the fact that "VIP" in VIP-Cre optogenetic experiments labels both anatomical subclasses, and the reported pyramidal IPSC is a weighted average of their opposing contributions {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a,Apicella2022,Pronneke2015`.

## Short-term plasticity biases disinhibitory gain

Short-term plasticity at VIP synapses has been measured in only a handful of studies, but what is measured is surprisingly consistent: VIP→SST synapses facilitate strongly, while other VIP edges and the inputs onto VIP show more variable dynamics. {cite:t}`Karnani2016a` characterize SST↔VIP synapses as "highly facilitating likely due to their slow kinetics" and conclude that such facilitating synapses are "ideal for promoting activity of population members on >100 ms timescales through inhibition of a reciprocally connected inhibitory population" {cite:p}`Karnani2016a`. {cite:t}`Pi2013` reports that SST and PV targets "showed similar short-term plasticity in ACx and mPFC" on single trains, with average IPSC amplitudes not significantly different between the two regions — suggesting that to first order the short-term filter of VIP output is conserved across areas {cite:p}`Pi2013`. {cite:t}`Pfeffer2013` makes this explicit: their between-class contribution estimates in Figure 4–7 rely on the assumption "that the short-term plasticity of transmitter release is similar between interneuron classes," an assumption they note is "largely the case, at least for Pvalb, Sst and VIP cells" {cite:p}`Pfeffer2013`. Where the literature does diverge is on excitatory drive onto VIP: {cite:t}`Karnani2016a` showed that PC→VIP connections in S1 are depressing (5/38 connections, 13%) with a similar organization in V1, whereas long-range bottom-up cortico-cortical drive onto VIP has been reported to show short-term facilitation {cite:p}`Ma2021`. Together these measurements imply that when a cortical area receives sustained top-down cortico-cortical drive, facilitating excitation of VIP and facilitating VIP→SST transmission will compound — biasing disinhibitory gain toward sustained rather than transient regimes — while brief local pyramidal volleys, delivered through a depressing PC→VIP synapse, will not recruit the disinhibitory path as effectively {cite:p}`Karnani2016a,Ma2021`. The mechanistic implication is clear but the quantitative comparison across studies is not available from this evidence package.

## Excitatory drive onto VIP: local, long-range, and neuromodulatory

{numref}`fig-sec6-drive-source-taxonomy` organizes the seven studies whose captions identify a specific drive source onto VIP (or, for {cite:t}`Obermayer2019`, VIP as the source of cholinergic release rather than the target). Four source categories recur. Local pyramidal drive was characterized in {cite:t}`Karnani2016a` via paired recordings of L2/3 PCs and VIP cells in S1 and V1, with 50 Hz trains demonstrating the depressing short-term dynamics discussed above; the same study includes VIP→VIP electrical coupling and reciprocal SOM↔VIP connections in the reported matrix {cite:p}`Karnani2016a`. Cholinergic drive — by far the most frequently invoked excitatory input onto VIP in the in-vivo literature — is supported by two independent mechanisms. {cite:t}`Chen2015` showed that basal-forebrain ChAT-ChR2 photostimulation elicited facilitating responses in L2/3 VIP cells in V1 (with parallel SOM→VIP inhibitory input), providing the fast-cholinergic evidence for VIP recruitment during arousal {cite:p}`Chen2015`. {cite:t}`Askew2019` demonstrated with bath nicotine that the nicotinic component selectively depolarized pyramidal and VIP cells in neocortical slices, consistent with β2*-nAChR expression on VIP {cite:p}`Askew2019`. {cite:t}`Cauli2014` integrate the pharmacology and physiology: VIP cells express functional nicotinic and serotonergic (5-HT3A) receptors, and Cauli and colleagues' own data place VIP among the cortical interneurons most strongly recruited by both acetylcholine and serotonin {cite:p}`Cauli2014`. Although the evidence package for Section 6 does not contain a dedicated serotonin-onto-VIP direct test, the 5-HT3A lineage assignment reviewed by {cite:t}`Rudy2010` and the arousal-correlated recruitment reported by {cite:t}`Fu2014` together place serotonergic drive as a second major neuromodulatory recruit pathway {cite:p}`Rudy2010,Fu2014,Cauli2014`.

A distinctive finding with no analog in other cortical interneuron classes is that VIP contains a ChAT-co-expressing subclass that RELEASES acetylcholine. {cite:t}`Obermayer2019` used whole-cell recordings in mPFC to show that ChAT-VIP cells produce fast cholinergic postsynaptic responses on neighboring pyramidal cells and are themselves sources of local ACh — making this VIP subclass both cholinergic target and cholinergic source {cite:p}`Obermayer2019`. This is why {numref}`fig-sec6-drive-source-taxonomy` represents Obermayer as a "VIP as source" (R) entry rather than a drive-onto-VIP entry. {cite:t}`Porter1999` is the original pharmacological basis for fast cholinergic excitation of VIP — "rapid depolarization" of GAD-positive bipolar cells by ACh — a classic result that anticipated the ChAT-VIP sub-lineage {cite:p}`Porter1999`. The molecular identity of the ChAT-VIP subclass is revisited in {ref}`sec-molecular-identity` and the circuit consequences in {ref}`sec-circuit-motifs`; for this section the relevant point is that "cholinergic modulation of VIP" is not a single phenomenon — fast nicotinic drive ONTO VIP and local ACh release BY ChAT-VIP operate on different timescales and have different postsynaptic targets.

Long-range cortico-cortical and thalamocortical drive onto VIP is the third broad input category. {cite:t}`Ma2021` mapped hierarchical CC/TC projections onto V1 interneuron populations and reported that top-down CC inputs produce the strongest responses in VIP+ cells (with synaptic depression), while bottom-up inputs preferentially drive PV+ neurons with initially weak but facilitating synapses onto VIP+ — a pattern of hierarchy-dependent, interneuron-type-specific innervation consistent with conditional disinhibitory amplification {cite:p}`Ma2021`. {cite:t}`Kastli2020` quantified photostim-driven laminar input onto superficial VIP+ and SST+ cells in barrel cortex and reported that VIP+ cells receive roughly equal excitatory input from all layers, whereas SST+ cells are driven chiefly from within L2/3 and from lateral sources — supporting a compartment-specific input geometry for VIP that differs from SST, though the paper does not directly compare VIP with PV {cite:p}`Kastli2020`. {cite:t}`Anastasiades2021b` locates a thalamo-mPFC route by which medio-dorsal thalamus preferentially activates VIP+ interneurons in mPFC that inhibit SST cells, extending the canonical VIP→SST arrangement to a thalamocortical-input-gated version in an associative area {cite:p}`Anastasiades2021b`. These three studies are consistent, and collectively make the case that the "drive onto VIP" pattern is dominated by long-range excitation rather than by local pyramidal recurrence. But note the denominator: three directly relevant studies out of dozens that invoke long-range drive onto VIP as the substrate for top-down disinhibition.

Finally, hippocampal and entorhinal-cortex data extend the drive taxonomy to a circuit setting where the VIP class is anatomically distinct. {cite:t}`Francavilla2018a` described a CA1 VIP long-range-projecting (VIP-LRP) subpopulation whose outputs are distinct from the classical cortical VIP bipolar / basket dichotomy: VIP-LRPs are theta-OFF in vivo and send long-range axons to the subiculum {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a`. {cite:t}`Bilash2023` describes a local entorhinal-driven disinhibitory VIP microcircuit in CA1 {cite:p}`Bilash2023`, and {cite:t}`Leroy2021` reports that hippocampal VIP interneurons in CA2 inhibit other interneurons and release enkephalin {cite:p}`Leroy2021` — both extending the VIP class into entorhinal–hippocampal circuitry without providing evidence for direct VIP inhibition of CA3 pyramidal cells. The hippocampal literature therefore extends the cortical disinhibitory role of VIP into an independent anatomical context; whether a minority direct VIP→pyramidal component is also present in hippocampus is not established by the studies in this evidence package.

## Context-dependence: when the motif breaks down in vivo

The motif-level in-vivo literature is the hinge between Section 6 and Section 7, and it contains the best-documented conflict in this section's evidence package. The classical VIP-disinhibition model, in which locomotion {cite:p}`Fu2014` or reinforcement signals {cite:p}`Pi2013` recruit VIP cells that then inhibit SST (and PV) and so disinhibit pyramidal cells, is drawn from two complementary studies: locomotion-driven disinhibition is the contribution of {cite:t}`Fu2014`, while reinforcement-driven disinhibition is the contribution of {cite:t}`Pi2013`. {cite:t}`Pakan2016` and {cite:t}`Dipoppa2018`, both recording in V1 with visual stimuli present, find that SST and PV cells are not uniformly suppressed by locomotion when visual input is driving the network — in some regimes SST activity INCREASES with locomotion, exactly the opposite of the VIP→SST-disinhibition prediction {cite:p}`Pakan2016,Dipoppa2018,Fu2014,Pi2013`. The evidence package flags six pairwise conflicts on this context-dependence axis (Fu2014 vs Pakan2016; Fu2014 vs Dipoppa2018; Pi2013 vs Pakan2016; Pi2013 vs Dipoppa2018; Kuchibhotla2016 vs Pakan2016; Kuchibhotla2016 vs Dipoppa2018), with a partial resolution: "the VIP→SST motif holds in darkness but is modified by visual context; recurrent interactions and changes to feedforward weights are required in the presence of stimuli." This is not the dismissal it first appears to be — both sides of the conflict are experimentally robust, and the resolution is that the motif is recruited conditionally on cortical state and sensory drive. But it does mean that the bare statement "VIP→SST disinhibition implements arousal modulation" is a context-stripped version of a more constrained claim {cite:p}`Fu2014,Pi2013,Pakan2016,Dipoppa2018`.

A sharper test comes from {cite:t}`MyersJoseph2024`, whose direct all-optical bidirectional manipulation during cross-modal attention in V1 found that attention modulation and VIP disinhibition share the same neurons but produce ORTHOGONAL patterns of circuit activity — attention is not implemented by VIP-SST disinhibition in the canonical sense {cite:p}`MyersJoseph2024`. This opposes the attention-by-VIP-disinhibition narrative propagated in reviews such as {cite:t}`GuetMcCreight2020` and marks the most important unresolved challenge to the generalization of the motif into a causal mechanism of cortical attentional modulation {cite:p}`GuetMcCreight2020,MyersJoseph2024`. A parallel challenge comes from {cite:t}`Khoury2023`, whose SST recordings across cortical areas (imaged on alternating days in auditory cortex and posterior parietal cortex) found that arousal and locomotion modulate SST activity differently across the two areas — i.e., arousal does not impose a uniform modulation on SST activity across areas, which is harder to reconcile with a single canonical VIP→SST→PYR circuit than with area-specific configurations {cite:p}`Khoury2023`. {cite:t}`Canetta2020` in mPFC adds yet another twist: in L2/3 mediodorsal-thalamus (MD) inputs preferentially target PV+ interneurons while ventral-hippocampal inputs preferentially target VIP+ interneurons, with MD inputs reported as more reliable at 20 Hz — showing that long-range inputs partition across interneuron classes rather than demonstrating layer-specific selective recruitment of VIP-mediated disinhibition per se {cite:p}`Canetta2020`. Together with the Pakan/Dipoppa/Myers-Joseph data, these results support the narrower formulation adopted by Section 7: VIP→SST disinhibition is one of several conditional motifs that VIP cells can participate in, and its recruitment depends on sensory drive, cortical state, and layer-specific long-range input composition {cite:p}`Fu2014,Pi2013,Pakan2016,Dipoppa2018,MyersJoseph2024,Khoury2023,Canetta2020`.

## Gap-junction coupling among VIP cells

Electrical coupling among VIP cells is the least-tested element of VIP connectivity, but the three direct measurements that exist agree qualitatively. {cite:t}`Karnani2016a` observed electrical coupling "only within VIP, SOM or PV populations" — intra-class, not cross-class — "in agreement with previous findings from populations excluding neurogliaform cells" {cite:p}`Karnani2016a`. Karnani's pharmacology showed that during GABA-receptor blockade SOM firing rate rose by 16 ± 7% above baseline; the equivalent effect on VIP cooperativity was not consistently observed, so the evidence for gap-junction-mediated cooperative firing among VIP cells from this study is weaker than the SOM result {cite:p}`Karnani2016a`. {cite:t}`Francavilla2018a` directly counted VIP-LRP pairs in CA1 and found that 16 of 36 attempted pairs (44%) were electrically coupled with a coupling coefficient of 0.11 ± 0.1; one pair was additionally synaptically connected. The same study showed, with modeling, that theta-modulated input to a gap-junction-coupled VIP-LRP network produces weak synchrony because spike after-hyperpolarizations propagate preferentially through the junctions, producing surround inhibition — a counterintuitive computational role for gap junctions that has not been tested in cortical VIP {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a`. {cite:t}`Scheyltjens2016` summarize the broader literature: SST, PV, and VIP interneurons are each electrically connected within their own subpopulations, with gap junctions allowing "the direct passage of ions and molecules smaller than 10 kDa, mediating the bidirectional coupling of metabolic and electrical activities," a result consistent across rodent cortical interneuron classes {cite:p}`Scheyltjens2016`. {cite:t}`GuetMcCreight2020` points to the additional complication that irregular-spiking VIP (I-S1) cells are reported to form "dendro-dendritic and axo-dendritic connections with themselves with some indication of gap junction connections" — i.e., VIP intra-class coupling may be partitioned by intrinsic subtype, although this has not been tested systematically {cite:p}`GuetMcCreight2020`. The literature therefore supports intra-class VIP electrical coupling as a reproducible finding but leaves the population-level consequences — whether VIP cells fire as a synchronous ensemble, or whether coupling primarily imposes surround inhibition — undetermined {cite:p}`Karnani2016a,Francavilla2018a,Scheyltjens2016,GuetMcCreight2020`.

## Conflict ledger: what remains unresolved

Four axes of unresolved disagreement recur across Section-6 findings, each anchored in the conflict tables of this section's evidence package.

First, whether VIP directly inhibits pyramidal cells as a reproducible minority outcome or only via disinhibition is unresolved across two explicit conflict pairs ({cite:t}`Pi2013` vs {cite:t}`Takesian2018`; {cite:t}`Kuchibhotla2016` vs {cite:t}`Takesian2018`). Each pair reports rigorous within-study evidence; each pair does not test the same pyramidal subpopulation, so the conflict may be partly attributable to which pyramidal subclass or cortical layer is sampled — with {cite:t}`Kuchibhotla2016` illustrating one layer/subpopulation rather than adjudicating sampling-dependent discrepancies {cite:p}`Pi2013,Takesian2018,Kuchibhotla2016`. The CCK-VIP anatomy of {cite:t}`Francavilla2018a` and {cite:t}`Apicella2022` provides a plausible mechanistic substrate — VIP-basket/CCK cells would generate the direct component that VIP-bipolar/CR cells would not — but this decomposition has not been tested with subtype-specific optogenetics {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a,Apicella2022`.

Second, the context-dependence of locomotion-driven disinhibition is unresolved between the {cite:t}`Fu2014`/{cite:t}`Pi2013` classical model and the {cite:t}`Pakan2016`/{cite:t}`Dipoppa2018` visual-stimulus modifications. The evidence package flags the partial resolution — darkness vs visual-context regimes — as robust, but the broader claim that a single cortical motif implements arousal modulation is directly opposed by {cite:t}`MyersJoseph2024`, whose cross-modal-attention data found VIP-disinhibition and attention to be orthogonal in V1 {cite:p}`Fu2014,Pi2013,Pakan2016,Dipoppa2018,MyersJoseph2024`. Third, the programmed-cell-death mechanism for VIP interneurons — by extrapolation from the glutamatergic activity-dependent survival demonstrated for MGE-derived interneurons by {cite:t}`Wong2018`, versus the serotonin-mediated activity-dependent survival of CGE-derived bipolar cells reported by {cite:t}`Wong2022` — is only partially resolved, with implications that carry into Section 6 via the developmental regulation of the VIP cell number that ultimately sets the connectivity matrix density {cite:p}`Wong2018,Wong2022`.

Fourth, the cross-study quantitative synthesis that a canonical-motif claim would require — a connection-probability and IPSC-amplitude table across VIP→SST/PV/Pyr/VIP edges spanning Pfeffer, Pi, Karnani, Jiang, Melzer and modern large-scale datasets ({cite:t}`Campagnola2022`, {cite:t}`SchneiderMizell2025`) — is not available from the captions in this section's evidence package. The test-coverage matrix in {numref}`fig-sec6-target-coverage-matrix` is what the current extracted evidence licenses; a quantitative connectivity matrix across studies is a deferred goal that will require paper-level text extraction beyond figure captions. This is a methodological caveat, but it also reflects a real limitation of the literature: the few quantitative cross-lab connectivity tables that exist (e.g., the Pfeffer summary, the Jiang reconstruction) are not interchangeable because of differences in target subtype labeling, train paradigm, and IPSC measurement convention. A single consensus matrix for the VIP→interneuron fields does not yet exist.

## Synthesis and forward pointer

What this section's evidence base supports, read strictly, is a smaller claim than the canonical narrative suggests. VIP output preferentially reaches SST and PV targets in slice, with a small but reproducible direct-to-pyramidal component reported in some studies, as visualized in {numref}`fig-sec6-target-coverage-matrix` {cite:p}`Pfeffer2013,Pi2013,Melzer2021,Takesian2018`; whether the magnitude of the direct-to-pyramidal component depends on VIP sub-lineage remains a hypothesis consistent with the anatomy reviewed in {cite:t}`Francavilla2018a` rather than a directly tested finding in this evidence package. VIP cells receive hierarchy-dependent long-range input, with bottom-up cortico-cortical drive reported as short-term-facilitating onto VIP and top-down drive reported as strongest (though depressing) onto VIP ({cite:p}`Ma2021,Anastasiades2021b`), by cholinergic input from basal forebrain ({cite:p}`Chen2015,Askew2019`), and in the ChAT-VIP sub-lineage by an autocrine cholinergic mechanism in which VIP itself releases acetylcholine onto nearby pyramidal cells ({cite:p}`Obermayer2019,Porter1999`). Short-term plasticity is facilitating at VIP→SST and depressing at local PC→VIP, biasing the disinhibitory path toward sustained top-down regimes ({cite:p}`Karnani2016a,Ma2021`). Intra-class electrical coupling is present but its population consequences are unresolved ({cite:p}`Karnani2016a,Francavilla2018a,Scheyltjens2016`). The in-vivo recruitment of the motif is conditional on sensory drive, cortical state, and layer-specific long-range drive; the strongest causal claim — that VIP disinhibition implements cortical attention — is directly challenged by {cite:t}`MyersJoseph2024`, and in-vivo modulation of SST by arousal and locomotion has been shown to be region-specific rather than uniform {cite:p}`Khoury2023`, consistent with conditional recruitment of inhibitory motifs (though the VIP→SST motif itself was not directly tested in {cite:t}`Khoury2023`) {cite:p}`MyersJoseph2024,Fu2014,Pi2013,Pakan2016,Dipoppa2018,Khoury2023`.

The circuit-motifs section, {ref}`sec-circuit-motifs`, pushes on the consequence: given that the connectivity substrate reported here is heterogeneous, region-dependent, and only partially quantified, how robustly does the VIP→SST→PYR motif generalize across cortical areas, layers, and behavioral contexts? The answer — and the implications for a review of "VIP interneurons as universal disinhibitors" — is taken up there. The in-vivo-processing section, {ref}`sec-in-vivo-behavior`, extends the neuromodulatory drive argument into behavior, and The computational-models section, {ref}`sec-computational-models`, is where the connectivity parameters compiled here (and the ones not yet compiled) become the scaffolding of spiking-network models whose predictions we will then need to audit back against the thin direct-test base identified in {numref}`fig-sec6-target-coverage-matrix`.
