# Introduction

> "VIP cells inhibit SST cells, which inhibit pyramidal dendrites." A single sentence — repeated so often that it has begun to function as the *definition* of VIP interneurons, rather than as one empirical finding about a subset of them.

## Why a critical VIP interneuron review now

Vasoactive intestinal peptide (VIP)-expressing GABAergic interneurons are a numerical minority among cortical inhibitory cells. Across immunohistochemical {cite:p}`Rudy2010`, Cre-driver {cite:p}`Taniguchi2014,Pronneke2015`, and single-cell transcriptomic {cite:p}`Tasic2018` estimates, they comprise roughly 10–15% of cortical GABAergic neurons — a smaller fraction than parvalbumin (PV) basket cells or somatostatin (SST) Martinotti cells {cite:p}`Tremblay2016`. Yet within the last decade this minority class has acquired outsized theoretical weight. A concentrated cluster of landmark studies in mouse primary sensory and medial prefrontal cortex {cite:p}`Lee2013,Pfeffer2013,Pi2013,Fu2014` established that VIP interneurons preferentially inhibit other interneurons — chiefly SST cells in superficial layers, a smaller fraction of PV cells — and that activating VIP cells transiently releases pyramidal cell dendrites from SST-mediated inhibition. That circuit motif, often abbreviated **VIP → SST ⊣ PYR** or labelled "cortical disinhibition," became the engine of a general theoretical picture: VIP cells are the cortex's dedicated gain and context gate, recruited by arousal, locomotion, and reinforcement, acting uniformly across areas.

The framing has been productive. It organised the interpretation of top-down modulation {cite:p}`Zhang2014,Wang2018`, locomotor gain {cite:p}`Fu2014,Dipoppa2018,Pakan2016`, reinforcement-triggered plasticity {cite:p}`Letzkus2011,Krabbe2019`, and attention and contextual modulation of visual processing {cite:p}`MyersJoseph2022,Keller2020a`. It seeded an entire generation of network models in which VIP is one of three canonical inhibitory nodes (PV, SST, VIP) with fixed wiring and fixed functional role {cite:p}`Hertag2019,Hertag2020`. It shaped the interpretation of disease phenotypes in Rett {cite:p}`Mossner2020`, Dravet {cite:p}`BatistaBrito2017,Miralles2023`, and fragile-X {cite:p}`Goff2019,Goff2023` models where VIP cells were identified as loci of dysfunction.

The framing has also outrun the data that generated it. Single-cell transcriptomic atlases {cite:p}`Tasic2016,Tasic2018,Yao2021a,Bakken2021,BRAINInitiativeCellCensusNetworkBICCN2021` dissolve "VIP" into multiple transcriptomically distinct branches with different developmental trajectories, different morphologies, and different connectivity {cite:p}`Pronneke2015,Gouwens2020a,Gouwens2020b,Scala2020,Fuzik2015`. Hippocampal VIP interneurons {cite:p}`Acsady1996,Francavilla2018a,Francavilla2020,Luo2020,Tyan2014,Turi2019,Melzer2021` and deep-layer, associative, motor, and higher-order cortical VIP cells {cite:p}`Apicella2022,Anastasiades2021b` do not cleanly reproduce the superficial primary-sensory template. In-vivo recordings disagree on whether VIP activity primarily tracks reinforcement, sensory stimuli, or behavioural actions {cite:p}`Pi2013,Piet2024,Bae2025,Ramamurthy2023`. Human and cross-species data document subtypes with no obvious mouse homolog {cite:p}`Hodge2019,Bakken2021,Krienen2020,Krienen2023,Boldog2018`. And the disinhibitory motif itself, when tested head-on in attentional or contextual-modulation paradigms, has in several cases been shown to be orthogonal to, rather than the mechanism of, the behavioural gain it was supposed to mediate {cite:p}`MyersJoseph2022,MyersJoseph2024`.

The pressure to revise is therefore not ideological but empirical. This review treats the "universal disinhibitor" framing as a claim to be tested rather than as a premise to be assumed, and asks — across twelve evidence classes — what replicates, what is contested, and what is untested. The thesis that emerges, defended in the synthesis at {ref}`sec-synthesis` and reaffirmed at {ref}`sec-conclusion`, is that **VIP interneurons are a molecularly structured, CGE-derived inhibitory class whose circuit function is best predicted by the joint specification of subtype, region, layer, behavioural state, and molecular context. The canonical disinhibitory motif is one well-documented cell of that grid, not the grid itself.**

## From VIP peptide to interneuron class: a compressed history

The modern concept of "VIP interneuron" is the product of three partially overlapping research programs — peptide biochemistry, morphological neuroanatomy, and molecular genetics — whose convergence in the last fifteen years produced the object that this review surveys.

The peptide VIP was isolated from porcine intestine in the early 1970s as a 28-amino-acid vasoactive factor, and was subsequently shown to be distributed in neurons throughout the central nervous system, including cortical and hippocampal GABAergic cells. Early neuroanatomical work {cite:p}`Acsady1996,Cauli1997` established that cortical VIP⁺ neurons were largely bipolar or bitufted, often co-expressed calretinin (CR) or cholecystokinin (CCK), and — crucially — preferentially contacted other interneurons rather than pyramidal cells. {cite:t}`Acsady1996` specifically showed, in hippocampus, that VIP⁺ cells targeted GABAergic cells, providing the first anatomical substrate for a disinhibitory role. These observations, together with {cite:t}`Cauli1997`'s molecular-physiological profiling, situated VIP within the emerging catalogue of cortical interneuron types that also included PV basket and chandelier cells and SST Martinotti cells.

The second program — large-scale developmental and molecular genetics — reframed VIP interneurons as a member of a CGE-derived lineage distinct from the MGE-derived PV and SST branches {cite:p}`Butt2005,Lee2010,Miyoshi2010,Miyoshi2015,Lodato2011,Vucurovic2010,Mayer2018,Bandler2021,Allaway2021`. Lineage tracing from the caudal ganglionic eminence labelled VIP, CR, CCK, reelin⁺, and Lamp5 populations in a single developmental pool distinguishable from MGE derivatives from embryonic stages onward; the distinction was formalised transcriptomically by {cite:t}`Tasic2018` and {cite:t}`Yao2021a`, who placed the Vip subclass in a CGE-derived neighbourhood with Sncg and Lamp5 rather than with Pvalb and Sst. Selective Cre driver lines — especially the VIP-ires-Cre line {cite:p}`Taniguchi2014,Pronneke2015` — gave the field a genetic handle on the class that earlier immunohistochemical methods could not provide.

The third program — circuit-level optogenetics in awake behaving mice — turned that molecular handle into a functional story. {cite:t}`Lee2013` showed that VIP-mediated disinhibition is engaged during active processing in mouse somatosensory cortex during whisking; {cite:t}`Pfeffer2013` provided the first comprehensive inter-interneuron connectivity matrix demonstrating strong VIP→SST inhibition; {cite:t}`Pi2013` — arguably the single most-cited foundational paper in this area — showed that VIP cells in mouse auditory and prefrontal cortex are recruited by reinforcement signals and act as a generic "disinhibitory motif" for behaviourally relevant events; and {cite:t}`Fu2014` demonstrated that VIP interneurons in mouse V1 are strongly activated by locomotion, and that this activation drives a disinhibitory SST silencing that enhances visual responses. These four papers, more than any others, established the language and framing that this review re-examines.

The single-cell transcriptomic era {cite:p}`Tasic2016,Tasic2018,Yao2021a,Bakken2021,BRAINInitiativeCellCensusNetworkBICCN2021,Hodge2019,Scala2020,MunozManchado2018,Bhattacherjee2023` then complicated the picture. Molecular atlases — initially of mouse visual, motor, and anterior-lateral cortex; subsequently of mouse whole cortex and of human middle temporal gyrus — showed that the Vip subclass is not a single cell type but a branch containing multiple transcriptomic supertypes and a larger number of fine-grained clusters, including a distinct VIP/ChAT branch, a VIP long-range-projecting (VIP-LRP) branch documented morphologically and functionally in hippocampus {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a,Francavilla2018b,Francavilla2020,Luo2018,Luo2019,Luo2020`, and a still-being-characterised Id2/Sncg/Lamp5 neighbourhood {cite:p}`Tasic2018`. Patch-seq studies {cite:p}`Fuzik2015,Gouwens2020a,Gouwens2020b,Scala2020` have further suggested that within-branch boundaries may be continuous rather than categorical. The cell class implied by "VIP interneurons" is therefore not a single functional unit but a structured pool, with at least three developmentally and molecularly distinguishable branches and substantial within-branch variation.

## The universal-disinhibitor claim and what it predicts

The review's adjudication target is not any single empirical finding but a composite claim that has crystallised over roughly a decade. Stated in its strongest form, the **universal cortical disinhibitor framing** holds that:

1. **Targeting.** VIP interneurons preferentially inhibit other interneurons — principally SST cells, with a smaller projection to PV cells — rather than pyramidal neurons {cite:p}`Pfeffer2013,Pi2013,Lee2013`.
2. **Motif invariance.** This targeting pattern has been proposed as a cortex-wide default, across layers and areas, with only quantitative variation {cite:p}`Tremblay2016,Szadai2022`, on the basis of observations extending across auditory and medial prefrontal cortex {cite:p}`Pi2013`.
3. **Behavioural recruitment.** VIP activity is recruited by reinforcement {cite:p}`Pi2013`, locomotion {cite:p}`Fu2014`, arousal and attention {cite:p}`Kuchibhotla2016,Reimer2014`, and other "behaviourally relevant" signals delivered largely by neuromodulatory and long-range cortical inputs {cite:p}`Letzkus2011,Zhang2014,Wang2018`.
4. **Functional consequence.** The resulting VIP → SST ⊣ PYR circuit has been argued to act as a multiplicative gain term, originally characterised for reinforcement-modulated responses {cite:p}`Pi2013,Fu2014`, and proposed to extend to stimulus processing, attention, plasticity, and learning {cite:p}`Krabbe2019,Hertag2019,Veit2023`.

We use the shorthand **"canonical disinhibition motif"** throughout this review for the combination of claims 1–4, and we reserve the phrase **"universal cortical disinhibitor"** for the further claim that the motif, *as stated*, is the defining functional role of the class across cortex, states, and species.

Each claim in the composite is individually testable, and each has been tested — with mixed results. Claim 1 (VIP→SST preference) replicates robustly in mouse primary sensory cortex superficial layers under slice conditions {cite:p}`Pfeffer2013,Karnani2016a,CantoBustos2022,Wang2018,Rachel2025`, but translates imperfectly to deep layers and associative regions {cite:p}`Apicella2022,Anastasiades2021b`, and is complicated by VIP/ChAT, VIP-LRP, and hippocampal VIP subtypes whose postsynaptic targets differ {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a,Francavilla2020,Luo2020,Rhomberg2018`. Claim 2 (motif invariance) is contradicted by substantial connectome-level {cite:p}`MICrONSConsortium2025` and functional {cite:p}`Apicella2022` heterogeneity across areas and layers. Claim 3 (reinforcement/arousal coding) replicates for locomotion and global arousal {cite:p}`Fu2014,Dipoppa2018,Pakan2016,Millman2020,Lenkey2025` but is contested for reward and reinforcement signs {cite:p}`Piet2024,Bae2025,Ramamurthy2023` and for attentional gain {cite:p}`MyersJoseph2022,MyersJoseph2024`. Claim 4 (multiplicative gain) is strongly supported by computational models that assume it {cite:p}`Hertag2019,Hertag2020,Veit2023,Wagatsuma2022a,Wagatsuma2022b,Beerendonk2023` and weakly supported by direct tests of its behavioural necessity outside mouse V1 L2/3 {cite:p}`MyersJoseph2022,MyersJoseph2024`.

The net pattern across the four claims is that the framing is approximately right in the preparation in which it was developed — mouse primary sensory cortex layer 2/3 under defined arousal and task conditions — and approximately wrong when extrapolated beyond that preparation. A review of VIP interneurons in 2026 therefore must do two things simultaneously: (i) survey the evidence that supports the canonical motif in its native preparation, and (ii) document the boundary conditions that prevent its uncritical generalisation. This is the review's central task.

## Scope

This review covers VIP-expressing GABAergic interneurons of the mammalian cerebral cortex and hippocampus, with emphasis on mouse where the evidence is deepest and on human/primate where translational claims can be anchored {cite:p}`Hodge2019,Bakken2021,Krienen2020,Krienen2023,Boldog2018`. We treat VIP interneurons as a structured class and explicitly distinguish:

- **VIP interneurons** (the GABAergic class targeted by VIP-ires-Cre, VIP-Flp, and related intersectional strategies, comprising bipolar/CR⁺, basket/CCK⁺, ChAT⁺, and long-range-projecting branches);
- **VIP-expressing cells** more broadly (which include non-GABAergic or non-cortical VIP⁺ populations such as suprachiasmatic clock neurons or peripheral neurons — outside the review's scope);
- **VIP subtypes**, by which we mean transcriptomically- and/or morphologically-defined subdivisions within the interneuron class (the primary axis being VIP/CR⁺ bipolar vs VIP/CCK⁺ basket, with VIP/ChAT and VIP-LRP as additional branches).

We **do not cover** in depth: (a) peripheral VIP biology (vasoactive, endocrine, and gastrointestinal functions); (b) detailed peptide biochemistry and receptor (VPAC1/VPAC2/PAC1) pharmacology, except where directly relevant to circuit function; (c) non-mammalian VIP biology; (d) VIP-expressing non-neuronal cells; and (e) clinical intervention literature beyond what is needed to translate circuit-level findings into disease-level ones.

The review proceeds in twelve thematic body sections, a cross-cluster synthesis, and this introduction and its companion conclusion. Section {ref}`sec-molecular-identity` surveys the molecular and transcriptomic taxonomy of VIP interneurons and argues that the class is internally structured at multiple resolutions. Section {ref}`sec-development` follows the developmental trajectory from CGE progenitors to the adult class, with particular attention to the programs that specify bipolar/CR⁺ versus basket/CCK⁺ fate. Section {ref}`sec-morphology` catalogues morphological diversity and the Patch-seq-enabled mapping of morphology onto transcriptomic axes. Section {ref}`sec-electrophysiology` reviews intrinsic firing properties and their correspondence — partial, not bijective — to molecular types. Section {ref}`sec-synaptic-connectivity` surveys synaptic input and output, including the preferential VIP→SST motif and its qualifiers (short-term plasticity, quantal properties, convergence). Section {ref}`sec-circuit-motifs` examines local-circuit disinhibitory and inhibitory motifs across sensory, frontal, and motor areas, and asks whether the canonical motif is a cortex-wide template or a sensory-cortex regularity. Section {ref}`sec-in-vivo-behavior` synthesises in-vivo recording and perturbation studies across sensory processing, locomotion, arousal, reward, attention, and learning. Section {ref}`sec-regions` compares hippocampal, entorhinal, prefrontal, motor, and associative VIP populations to the primary-sensory template. Section {ref}`sec-oscillations` reviews VIP contributions to theta, gamma, ripple, and slow-oscillation dynamics. ## Adjudication strategy: replicated, contested, untested

The review's method is not a narrative summary; it is an evidence-grading exercise. Each major claim encountered in sections {ref}`sec-molecular-identity`–{ref}`sec-computational-models` is followed through to the replication status it has actually achieved. The tiers we use throughout, formalised in {ref}`sec-synthesis`, are:

- **Strong.** Three or more independent groups, using non-identical methods, report concordant results in comparable preparations, with no substantive negative replication.
- **Moderate.** Two independent replications, or one replication combined with methodological reuse in downstream studies.
- **Weak.** Single-lab claims or correlational evidence only.
- **Speculative.** Plausible on general circuit grounds but not directly measured at the required resolution.

Three additional adjudication categories are applied where relevant: **contested** (two or more published studies in direct disagreement on the same outcome variable), **untested** (a prediction of the canonical framework that has not been directly measured in the preparation the framework names), and **not generalisable** (a finding that is strongly replicated *within* a preparation but for which extrapolation beyond it has failed). This grading apparatus is applied to eight curated disagreements that run as threads through the review and are resolved — as far as current evidence allows — in {ref}`sec-synthesis`.

Readers who prefer a cartographic entry point can treat the review as organised around a subtype × region × layer × state grid. The rows and columns of that grid are defined by the evidence classes in sections {ref}`sec-molecular-identity`–{ref}`sec-computational-models`; the cells in the grid are the empirical claims. The canonical motif lives in a small, well-populated region of the grid (mouse primary sensory cortex, L2/3, high-arousal/active states, VIP/CR⁺ and VIP/CCK⁺ subtypes pooled). Most other regions of the grid are sparsely populated, and several are empty. The review makes this structure explicit; {ref}`sec-conclusion` converts it into an experimental agenda.

## Terminology and nomenclature

Three terminological choices deserve to be stated explicitly at the outset.

**"VIP interneuron" versus "VIP-expressing cell."** We use **VIP interneuron** for cortical and hippocampal GABAergic cells captured by VIP-ires-Cre-derived genetic labelling or by VIP immunohistochemistry combined with a GABAergic marker. We use **VIP-expressing cell** more broadly — for any cell with detectable VIP mRNA or protein, which includes populations outside the review's scope. The distinction matters because a nontrivial fraction of VIP-expressing cells in some regions are not classical interneurons (for example VIP long-range-projecting cells {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a,Luo2018`), and conversely because some "VIP-Cre"-captured cells express VIP only transiently during development {cite:p}`Taniguchi2014,Pronneke2015`.

**Subtype nomenclature.** We use **VIP/CR⁺ bipolar**, **VIP/CCK⁺ basket**, **VIP/ChAT⁺**, and **VIP long-range-projecting (VIP-LRP)** as the primary subtype axes, informed by the transcriptomic {cite:p}`Tasic2018,Yao2021a`, morphological/Patch-seq {cite:p}`Pronneke2015,Gouwens2020a,Gouwens2020b`, and VIP-LRP/hippocampal {cite:p}`Francavilla2018a,Francavilla2020,Luo2020` literature of the last decade. We use the slash convention (VIP/CR⁺) rather than hyphens to denote co-expression, and we follow the style-guide convention of the review body throughout.

**The "canonical disinhibition motif."** Whenever this phrase appears, it refers specifically to the VIP → SST ⊣ PYR circuit as characterised in {cite:p}`Lee2013,Pfeffer2013,Pi2013,Fu2014` — that is, the motif together with the behavioural-recruitment claim that VIP activity is driven by reinforcement, locomotion, arousal, or top-down modulation. Where later authors use "canonical motif" in a weaker sense (preferential VIP→SST inhibition alone, without the behavioural claim), we call that the **connectivity motif** and keep it separate. Sections {ref}`sec-synaptic-connectivity`, {ref}`sec-circuit-motifs`, and {ref}`sec-in-vivo-behavior` adjudicate these components separately.

Throughout the review we follow the Phase-10 terminology decisions recorded in the project style guide: **VIP interneurons** as the canonical form for the class (with "VIP⁺" also acceptable); **L2/3** for layer 2/3; **irregular-spiking** (hyphenated) for the intrinsic firing pattern; and **CGE-derived** (preferred over "caudal ganglionic eminence–derived" outside first-use definitions). We retain **SST** in preference to **SOM** except in direct quotations and in composite labels (e.g., Pyr/PV/SST/VIP) that would fragment if renamed.

## Reading guide

The review is designed to be readable either sequentially or as a reference. Readers approaching the topic for the first time are encouraged to read sections {ref}`sec-molecular-identity`, {ref}`sec-development`, {ref}`sec-synaptic-connectivity`, {ref}`sec-in-vivo-behavior`, {ref}`sec-synthesis`, and {ref}`sec-conclusion` as a minimum, in that order. Readers with a specific question — for example, whether the canonical motif generalises to their preparation of interest — can jump to {ref}`sec-circuit-motifs` or {ref}`sec-regions` directly. Readers interested in computational modelling can read {ref}`sec-computational-models` as a standalone piece, though we note that its force is multiplied by first reading {ref}`sec-synthesis`, which defines the empirical targets that models should aim to match.

A note on citation practice. The canonical motif's citation genealogy is a textbook case of narrative compression: a large body of specific empirical claims — each bounded by a specific preparation, a specific set of cell types, a specific behavioural regime — has been repeatedly compressed, in successive review articles, into a small number of general statements. We have attempted throughout this review to quote primary evidence and to name the specific preparation in which a claim was established, rather than to perpetuate the compression. Where the review cites a later synthesis, we do so because the synthesis itself introduces a new claim or because it summarises a body of work too large to cite primary-only. The adjudications in {ref}`sec-synthesis` rest on the primary literature.

With the scope, thesis, and strategy stated, we turn in {ref}`sec-molecular-identity` to the molecular and transcriptomic foundation of the class — the first of the twelve evidence pillars this review assembles, and the layer at which the internal structure of VIP interneurons first becomes unignorable.
